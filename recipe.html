Flatbread

Our recipe for January is Flatbread. For many of us, bread-making isn’t as easy as some people make it out to be! The truth is, making bread can be very time-consuming; when you’re stuck for time, it can seem so much easier to just buy your bread from the shop. Well, Flatbread might be your saviour! It’s a super-easy alternative to more complicated breads, and it’s also one that the kids can help out with.


Prepared and cooked in less than 15 minutes, flatbread goes well with soups, pasta dishes, fry-ups, or even just on its own. Made with just four ingredients, it’s also a much healthier alternative to many shop-bought breads.


For this recipe, we’re keeping it simple. But you can make things a little more interesting by adding your choice of herbs and spices. In our house, we like to add some crushed fresh garlic to the butter when we’re frying!


Ingredients

  • 100g Self-Raising Flour
  • 100g Natural Yoghurt
  • Pinch of Salt
  • Butter for Frying

Tools

  • Bowl and Fork for Mixing
  • Knife
  • Frying Pan
  • Basting Brush (not essential)
  • Eggslice for Flipping Action!

Instructions

  1. In a clean bowl, mix the flour, salt and yoghurt with a fork until the mixture comes together.
  2. Dust a clean surface lightly with flour, then using your hands roll the mixture into a sausage shape.
  3. Cut into four equal sizes.
  4. With a rolling pin lightly dusted with flour, or the palm of your hand, flatten the four pieces into a disc shape. Be careful not to use too much flour as this will burn on the pan.
  5. Add a knob of butter to a hot pan, place the flatbread disc onto the pan and cook for approximately 1 minute. While the bottom side is cooking place another knob of butter on the top side to melt, using a basting brush to spread or just let it melt.
  6. Flip the flatbread and cook on the other side for another minute.
  7. Can be served immediately or eaten cold.
